# Commercial Licensing

This plugin is free for **noncommercial use** under [CC BY-NC-SA 4.0](./LICENSE). If your intended use is commercial, a separate commercial license is required.

## Who needs a commercial license

Anyone who uses this plugin as part of **paid development work** or **revenue-generating activity**. Typical cases:

- Employees at for-profit companies using the plugin to help build the company's products
- Contractors / consultants using the plugin on paid engagements
- Individuals shipping derivative works (forks, plugins that include this one) commercially
- Anyone redistributing the plugin's materials in a commercial product or service

**Who doesn't:**

- Hobbyists building open-source software for personal or community projects
- Students and researchers using the plugin academically
- Anyone whose work with the plugin is not directed towards commercial advantage

The distinction follows CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 §1(k): *"NonCommercial means not primarily intended for or directed towards commercial advantage or monetary compensation."*

## What a commercial license grants

Typical terms in the commercial license:

- Permission to use the plugin as part of commercial development work
- Permission to build derivative works and ship them commercially
- No ShareAlike requirement — your derivative stays under your chosen license
- Attribution retained for the original author
- Upstream third-party licenses (MIT, LGPL, MPL, etc.) pass through unchanged; the commercial license cannot override those

Pricing and specific terms are negotiated per-buyer. Volume, support needs, and scope of use are typical factors.

## What your own code status looks like — either way

**The software you build with help from the plugin is yours.** Whether you hold the default CC license or a commercial license, your own application code, your own formal models describing your system, and your own verification reports are not derivative works of this plugin (see the [derived-Claude-code-work analysis](./system-models/derived-claude-code-work/report.md) for the full technical argument).

What a commercial license unlocks is the **use** of the plugin itself in a commercial context, not the copyright status of the code you produce.

## How to request a commercial license

Email **contact@a-morozov.com** with a short description of:

- Your organization and the intended use
- Rough scope (one project, ongoing use, specific engagement)
- Any specific terms you'd need beyond the typical ones above

Expect a reply within a few business days with draft terms and a fee estimate.
